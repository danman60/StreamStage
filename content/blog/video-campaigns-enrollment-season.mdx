## Why enrollment season needs video

Fall and January enrollment periods often make or break a dance studio’s revenue. Parents sift through a sea of options, and your messaging must be clear, compelling and timely. Data from HubSpot shows that **93% of marketers see strong ROI from video marketing**, and short‑form video is the content type marketers plan to increase investment in. Video showcases your facility, faculty and community in ways text can’t, making it a cornerstone of an effective enrollment push.

## Map out your timeline

A successful campaign doesn’t happen overnight. Build a schedule that accounts for planning, production, editing and promotion.

1. **Six to eight weeks before registration opens** – Brainstorm your campaign theme. Will you emphasise community, performance excellence or beginner friendliness? Identify the types of videos you need: a flagship promo, class previews, testimonials and short social clips.
2. **Four to six weeks out** – Film your content. Remember that quality matters. Allow time to reschedule if key teachers or students are unavailable. Secure music licenses and collect release forms.
3. **Two to four weeks out** – Edit your videos. Keep social clips under one minute for higher engagement and ensure your promo reel is no longer than 90 seconds. Add captions for silent viewers.
4. **Two weeks out** – Schedule posts and ads. Create a content calendar with post dates, captions and target audiences. Coordinate email and website updates to coincide with your video release.
5. **Launch week** – Publish your main promo and class previews. Start paid campaigns on social platforms. Use Stories and Reels for reminders and behind‑the‑scenes snippets.
6. **During enrollment** – Maintain momentum with testimonials, live Q&As and countdown posts. Repurpose your footage into shorter clips for TikTok or Reels.

## Choose the right ad formats

Different platforms offer various ways to showcase video. Consider:

- **Facebook and Instagram ads** – Ideal for reaching parents. Use vertical or square formats and short copy. Test different audiences—by location, age of children and interests—and let the algorithm optimize delivery.
- **YouTube pre‑roll ads** – These play before other videos. Keep them under 15 seconds and front‑load your message. Target by geographic area and search behaviour (e.g., “kids activities,” “dance classes”).
- **TikTok Spark Ads** – Promote organic posts to new audiences. Ensure the first second is attention‑grabbing and relevant to TikTok’s trend‑driven environment.
- **Email video teasers** – Embed a thumbnail that links to your promo on your site. Video thumbnails in email can increase click‑through rates.

Use A/B testing where possible. HubSpot reports that companies often test different versions of a video or call‑to‑action; small changes in text or footage can significantly impact conversion.

## Target strategically

Define your audience segments and tailor messaging accordingly:

- **New students** – Showcase beginner classes and community atmosphere. Highlight trial offers or starter packages.
- **Returning dancers** – Emphasise progression opportunities: advanced classes, competition teams, new styles.
- **Parents of toddlers** – Use gentle imagery and testimonials about nurturing instruction. Address safety and separation anxiety.

Layer geographic targeting (by postal code or radius) with demographic filters (children’s ages, parents’ interests) to ensure your budget goes toward high‑potential viewers. Retarget website visitors who watched your videos but didn’t register; many platforms allow you to show follow‑up ads to people who engaged with your content.

## Create compelling creatives

Your video doesn’t need to go viral; it needs to resonate with your audience. Keep these principles in mind:

- **Hook immediately** – Start with exciting movement, smiling faces or a compelling question. People decide whether to keep watching within seconds.
- **Tell a story** – Show progress: a child walking into class shyly, then confidently performing. Emotional narratives build connection.
- **Include a clear call to action** – “Book your free trial,” “Register by [date] to secure your spot.” Make it easy for viewers to take the next step.
- **Respect attention spans** – Social clips should be brief; website videos can be slightly longer but still concise.

## Measure and adjust

Track views, click‑through rates, registrations and cost per acquisition. If one ad set performs poorly, pause it and reallocate budget. Review comments and messages to see what questions people are asking; address those questions in follow‑up videos or posts.
