## SEGMENT 1 — FIVE FREE AI MOVES (~9 min, ~1.5–2 min each)

*(Delivery rule for all five: name the pain → show the move → say the Monday action in one sentence. Keep the pattern audible: every tip is "teach it YOUR stuff once, then it works forever." That pattern IS the setup for StudioSage.)*

**TIP 1 — Talk, don't type.**
Open ChatGPT — the free one is fine. Now here's the single biggest unlock, and it's a button: the microphone. Stop typing. Hit the mic and *ramble* like you're leaving a voice memo for a friend — "okay so costume payments are late again and I need to remind parents without sounding annoyed for the third time." The second you start typing, it feels like homework and you quit. Talking is how you'll actually use this daily. **Monday move:** put the app on your phone's first screen, use the mic, never type again.

**TIP 2 — The newsletter that writes itself.**
Are we still sitting down writing the monthly newsletter by hand? Here's the whole fix. Feed ChatGPT your last fifty newsletters — copy, paste, done — and say: "make me a template in this voice." It learns YOUR voice — the way you sign off, the way you hype the recital. Now every month you hit the mic and *speak* the updates — costumes due the 8th, photos Saturday — and it writes the newsletter, in your voice. You read it, you fix one word so it feels like yours, you hit send. Ten minutes instead of two hours. **Monday move:** paste in the old newsletters, save the template, done forever. Do the hard thing once — hear the theme yet?

**TIP 3 — The schedule detangler.**
Rehearsal scheduling. Staring at a wall of dates, three rooms, nine teachers, and Miss Emma can't do Tuesdays. Paste the whole ugly mess in — teacher availability, room list, class list — and ask: "find the conflicts and propose three schedules." It won't be perfect. It'll be *ninety seconds to a draft* instead of a Sunday night. You fix the draft; fixing is faster than staring. **Monday move:** next scheduling headache, paste it in raw before you touch the whiteboard.

**TIP 4 — Reply drafts in your voice.**
The email you rewrite four times — the delicate one. Costume fee dispute, a parent upset about placement. Paste in the parent's email plus two examples of how you've handled similar ones before, and say "draft my reply, same tone." You are NOT sending what it writes. You're editing draft one instead of staring at a blank box at 11pm — and it never comes out passive-aggressive, which, at 11pm, cannot be said for all of us. **Monday move:** save one good example of your "hard email" tone somewhere you can paste from.

**TIP 5 — Ask your own handbook.**
This one's the sleeper, and watch it closely because it comes back. Take your parent handbook PDF — the one nobody reads — and upload it into the chat. Now ask it questions. "What's the refund policy?" "When are costume fees due?" It answers *from your handbook*. Not from the internet — from YOUR document. *(beat)* You just taught an AI your studio's rules in one upload. Keep that move in your head. **Monday move:** upload the handbook, ask it the three questions parents ask most, watch it nail them.

*(Transition line:)* Notice what all five have in common. None of them made the AI smart *in general*. You made it smart about **your** studio — your voice, your schedule, your handbook — once. That's the entire trick. Now let me show you where it goes when you take it seriously.

*Hold whatever they say — you'll point back at it in the close. Now pay off the open loop.*

**Pay off the loop.** Remember the email I answered without typing, mid-livestream? Here's what happened. I'm running a competition — eight camera feeds, a tracking camera, an operator yelling in my ear — and a parent emails: "I never got the link." Now... they got the link. They did. I checked. They did, though. But they're stressed, and I cannot stop. So I'd built a little email spider. It reads the message, checks my calendar for which event I'm at *that day*, finds the right stream link, drafts the reply, scores its own confidence — "I'm 90% sure this is the answer" — fires it, and pings me so I see it went. Parent helped in seconds. I never touched my keyboard. I was, at the time, busy.

**I built it for my own pain first.** And your version of that email is sitting in your inbox right now: "What time is Saturday rehearsal?" "Classes during March break?" "Dress code for picture day?" You answer those a hundred times a year. You did not open a dance studio to be a receptionist.

**Pull, don't push.** And here's why blasting *more* info doesn't fix it. You email it, you text it, it's on the BAND app, it's on the website — and parents are still buried. My wife runs her own business, she's sharp, and on recital day she is texting the studio owner a question whose answer is — I promise you — sitting in her own inbox. I've watched it happen. I live with it. It's not carelessness; being *pushed* info means it's in a pile somewhere. People want to **pull**: ask one place, get the answer, now. That's the move. And remember tip five — you already know an AI can answer from *your* documents. So what if your parents could text that handbook?

**That's what we productized — StudioSage.** The AI front desk for your dance studio. Parents **text a number.** No app, no login — if they can text, they can use it, which means even the dad who still types with two fingers is covered. And it doesn't make things up. It answers from **your** stuff — tip five, all grown up.

**[LIVE ANIMATED DEMO — the KB builds in front of them]** Watch this. Now a parent texts "what time is Saturday rehearsal?" and — *(live reply appears)* — there it is, in the studio's own words. It didn't get smart in general. It got smart about *your* studio. Setup is about ten minutes. One class period, and you've trained your front desk.

**Same human-in-the-loop you just heard on the livestream.** When it's not confident, it does NOT guess. It flags the question in a **morning digest** — you glance at your phone, one-tap the answer, and it learns it forever. Exact same confidence-and-flag mechanic as my email spider. You're never out of the loop. You're just out of the *repetition*.

**[DEMO — get them texting]** Pull out your phones. Text this number right now, ask it anything a parent would. *(Let real questions hit the live screen. Show one land instantly, and if one flags, even better — "see, it didn't fake it.")*
