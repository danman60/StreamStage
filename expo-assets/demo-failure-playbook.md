# Demo Failure Playbook — Expo Talks
One page. Per failure: what you'll see → what to do → what to SAY. The audience only remembers a failure if you panic; every recovery line keeps the room.

## Talk 2, Slide 14 — KB animated demo
| Failure | Do | Say |
|---|---|---|
| Tab crashed / closed | Reopen `kb-demo/index.html` from bookmark or USB; `R` resets | "Even the demo warms up first." |
| Beat stuck / mis-clicked | `R` → restart takes 15 seconds, narrate over it | "Let me run that again — worth seeing twice." |
| Laptop dies entirely | Skip to describing over Slide 14's static frame; the deck slide carries the beats | "Picture it: empty brain → your emails → your handbook → instant answer. Ten minutes of setup." |

**Design guarantee:** the KB demo is a local file with zero network calls. Venue wifi CANNOT kill it. Only a dead laptop can — and the USB stick + any borrowed laptop with a browser recovers it.

## Talk 2, Slide 15 — audience texting the live number
| Failure | Do | Say |
|---|---|---|
| Wall won't load (venue wifi) | Phone hotspot (pre-staged) → reload wall | "Trade-show wifi, everyone's favorite sponsor." |
| Wall loads, texts not appearing | Audience replies still arrive on THEIR phones — ask people to read answers out loud | "Forget my screen — someone read out what it just told you." |
| Carrier delay (texts slow) | Keep talking; circle back after Slide 16 — messages will have landed | "It's faster than your front desk at 11pm, slower than a live stage. We'll check back." |
| A question flags instead of answering | NOTHING IS WRONG — this is the trust beat | "See that? It didn't know, so it didn't fake it. That's the whole point." |
| Nobody texts | Text it yourself from the podium phone (it's onboarded from rehearsal) | "Fine, I'll be the shy parent." |

**Never** apologize twice for the same failure. One line, move on.

## Talk 1, Slide 10 — content-day reel
| Failure | Do | Say |
|---|---|---|
| No audio over PA | Play muted, narrate the beats live — you know the reel cold | "Director's commentary version — even better." |
| Video won't play | USB backup copy; worst case describe the four deliverables on the slide list | "You'll have to book the live version." |

## Universal
- **Projector dies:** keep talking. Both talks are stories with slides, not slides with a narrator. The interaction beats (hands, shout-outs, texting) all work without a screen.
- **Clicker dies:** arrow keys. Stand at the laptop.
- **You blank:** the run-of-show card is in your pocket. Look down, breathe, next cue.

## The two-strike rule (adapted for stage)
Any live element that fails twice → switch to its fallback permanently for that session. Do not try a third time on stage; debug at the booth after.
